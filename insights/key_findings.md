# 📊 Key Business Findings & Recommendations

> Derived from SQL analysis of 3,000,888 sales records across 54 stores | Corporación Favorita, Ecuador (2013–2017)

---

## Finding 1 — Store Performance Is Heavily Concentrated in Quito

**What the data shows:**
- Store 44 (Quito) leads with $62.09M in total revenue
- 9 out of the top 10 stores are in Quito
- All top 10 stores are Type A format
- Store 51 in Guayaquil ($32.9M) is the only non-Quito store in the top 10

**Business Recommendation:**
The company's growth strategy should prioritize expanding Type A stores in Quito and replicating the operational model of Store 44 in other cities. Guayaquil's Store 51 shows strong potential — it ranks #1 in its city with $32.9M and could benefit from further investment.

---

## Finding 2 — GROCERY I Is the Undisputed Category Leader

**What the data shows:**
- GROCERY I generates $343.46M — the highest of all 33 product categories
- It is the top product in 53 out of 54 stores
- Average daily sales of GROCERY I ($3,776.97) are 58% higher than BEVERAGES ($2,385.79)
- Only Store 25 in Salinas breaks the pattern, where BEVERAGES leads

**Business Recommendation:**
GROCERY I should receive priority shelf space, procurement focus, and promotional budget. Store 25's deviation in Salinas suggests a local preference for beverages — this warrants a region-specific product strategy.

---

## Finding 3 — Promotions Drive 618% Higher Average Sales

**What the data shows:**
- Promoted items average $1,137.69 per record
- Non-promoted items average only $158.25 per record
- Only ~20% of all records (611,329 out of 3,000,888) involve promotions

**Business Recommendation:**
The ROI on promotions is exceptional. The company should consider increasing promotional coverage — especially in below-average stores and for slower-moving categories. Even a modest increase in promotion frequency could generate significant revenue uplift.

---

## Finding 4 — July Is the Peak Month; September Is the Weakest

**What the data shows:**
- July leads with $103.36M in total sales
- March ($97.23M) and December ($97.03M) are the next strongest months
- September is the weakest at $77.47M — a 25% drop from July
- There is a noticeable mid-year peak and an autumn dip pattern

**Business Recommendation:**
Inventory planning and staffing should be scaled up from June through August and in December. September and October require demand stimulation strategies — targeted promotions or special campaigns during this period could reduce the seasonal dip.

---

## Finding 5 — Holidays Generate 11.8% Higher Average Sales

**What the data shows:**
- Holiday average: $393.86 per transaction
- Non-holiday average: $352.16 per transaction
- Holiday uplift: +11.8% across 502,524 holiday records

**Business Recommendation:**
Holidays represent a reliable revenue boost. The company should ensure maximum stock availability, full staffing, and active promotions on national and regional holidays — particularly in Ecuador's key holiday periods like Carnival, Independence Day, and Christmas.

---

## Finding 6 — Strong 2014 Growth Followed by Stabilization

**What the data shows:**
- Most stores grew 40–65% in 2014 (first full year of expansion)
- Store 49 grew 94.08% in 2014
- Store 36 grew 136.97% in 2014 — likely a new store opening
- Growth slowed to 5–25% in 2015–2016
- 2017 shows negative growth across all stores — but only covers Jan–Aug, making it a partial year comparison

**Business Recommendation:**
The 2017 decline is a data artifact, not a real trend — the dataset ends in August 2017 and is being compared against full-year 2016 figures. For genuine trend analysis, 2013–2016 shows consistent growth, signaling a healthy business trajectory.

---

## Finding 7 — 35 Stores Underperform on Transaction Volume

**What the data shows:**
- Overall average daily transactions: 1,694.60
- Store 44 leads with 4,336.97 transactions/day — 2.56x the average
- Only 19 out of 54 stores are above the average
- The bottom stores average around 1,000–1,100 transactions/day

**Business Recommendation:**
The wide gap between top and bottom stores suggests operational inefficiencies or market underserving in lower-performing locations. Stores in smaller cities like Puyo (Store 22: 751/day) and Playas (Store 35: 670/day) may need localized marketing strategies or format reassessment.
